Conquer the Suffering: A Mixed-Reality Himalayan Soundscape

We are pleased to present Conquer the Suffering as a collaboration between Dr. Cecilia Wu and Daniel Schreck for consideration under the Rubin x Art Projects Grant Program. This project will synthesize Dr. Wu’s immersive soundscapes—grounded in Nepalese and Tibetan Buddhist ritual instruments, spiritual chants, and modern electronic composition—with Daniel’s immersive virtual-reality environments designed to capture Himalayan wisdom transmission for cross-platform spatial computing systems (with Unity, OpenXR, and RealityKit).

The project harnesses real-time Ambisonics audio to inspire immersive virtual-reality environments referencing Himalayan aesthetics and wisdom traditions. Whether manifested as mandala-like forms, subtle mountain vistas, or shifting gradients of light, each immersive environment developed for "Conquer the Suffering" will respond in tandem with the evolving music. This fusion will highlight themes of resilience and introspection within an artistic framework developed to harness modern technologies to support Himalayan wisdom traditions.

Conquer the Suffering emerges as a project that interweaves form along the axes of sonic and visual space, and that synthesizes traditional Himalayan contemplative practices with cutting-edge technology for experiential immersion. We believe this synergy aligns with the Rubin Museum’s dedication to preserving, celebrating, and transmitting Himalayan wisdom-culture in the modern age.

Technologies

Ambisonics

Ambisonics encodes a full 360-degree soundfield, capturing audio from every direction and maintaining spatial accuracy as the listener moves. This format integrates diverse inputs—vocals, instruments, and electronic elements—into a seamless sonic sphere. When synced with VR visuals, it heightens immersion and realism, allowing participants to experience nuanced shifts in perspective for a deeply meditative encounter.

Unity

Unity is a leading development platform for creating immersive graphical experiences that can be deployed across diverse headsets, including Oculus and Apple Vision Pro. Its flexible design tools, real-time rendering, and integrated VR libraries empower artists to craft dynamic virtual worlds, embedding interactive elements and rich lighting. This cross-platform reach ensures broad accessibility for transformative, visually engaging installations.

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality environments will support the presentation immersive experiences that respond dynamically to the composition’s soundscapes. By offering environments that are platform-agnostic and can run on headsets of the user's choice, Conquer the Suffering aims to transmit Himalayan wisdom culture through a multi-sensory journey that immerses the viewer into experiential landscapes of light and contemplation. The cross-platform reach of these environments supports broad accessibility to these wisdom spaces for years to come.

Potential Directions

We include the following set of potential environments to indicate the possible directions of artistic development. We envision Conquer the Suffering as a synthesis of several immersive virtual-reality environments with interwoven displays of light and sound.

A sunlit Tibetan plateau with rolling grasslands, white-walled monasteries, and fluttering prayer flags.

Shakyamuni Buddha glows with golden light, as shifting fractal patterns radiate from his luminous form.

Potential lines of development include environments that support interactivity with each of the Eight Auspicious Symbols.

VR environments offer a space to view—and, eventually, interact with—yidams (meditational dieties), such as 1000-armed Avalokiteśvara.

Contributors

Dr. Jiayue Cecilia Wu

Originally from Beijing, Dr. Jiayue Cecilia Wu (AKA: 武小慈) is a scholar, educator, musician, audio engineer, and technologist focusing on how music technology enhances the healing power of music. She worked as a professional musician, producer, and publishing manager at EMI Records and Universal Music Group for 10 years before she joined academia. She holds a B.S. in Design & Engineering, an M.A. in Music, Science, & Technology from Stanford University, and a Ph.D. in Media Arts &Technology from UCSB.

Daniel Schreck

Daniel Schreck is a M.S. candidate in the Computer Science department of Stanford University. Originally from New York City, he completed his Sc.B. in Neuroscience at Brown University, where he studied retinal neurobiology and visual processing in the Berson Lab and the neural correlates of meditative experience in the CLAN-Lab. Today, he's most interested in developing virtual reality environments for meditation.
